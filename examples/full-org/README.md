# Full Organization Example

A 10-agent company with 3 departments, initiative budgets, and trust evolution.

## Org Chart

```
CEO
├── VP Engineering
│   ├── Frontend Developer (ui team)
│   ├── Backend Developer (api team)
│   └── DevOps Engineer (infra team)
├── VP Sales
│   ├── Lead Gen Specialist (outreach team)
│   └── Account Executive (closing team)
└── Operations Lead
    └── Data Analyst
```

## Budget

- $150/day across the entire org ($300k/month cap)
- 10M tokens/day, $30/hour burst limit
- Product launch initiative: 40% ($60/day)
- Pipeline growth initiative: 30% ($45/day)
- Reserve for ad-hoc work: 30% ($45/day)

## What happens

1. **CEO** wakes daily, reviews all departments, allocates budget, sets priorities
2. **VP Engineering** plans sprint work, dispatches tasks to frontend/backend/devops
3. **VP Sales** manages pipeline, lead-gen runs on a daily cron
4. **Operations Lead** handles compliance, internal processes
5. Initiative budgets enforced — engineering can't overspend on product launch
6. Cascading budgets — CEO allocates to VPs, VPs allocate to their reports
7. Trust evolution — agents with strong track records earn more autonomy
8. Weekly digest shows: tasks completed, cost per department, initiative progress
